Information on this map

This map shows all emissions of CO and CO2 in Europe according to the E-PRTR. For more detail on the E-PRTR data and how it was obtained, read the section "Data" below. The tabs on the left allow to filter those emissions to obtain possible replication sites for a Carbon4PUR plant. The first tab contains filters based on the type of emissions, the second tab filters based on possible plants.

Legend

CO emissions are displayed with a yellow border and all CO2 emissions with an orange border. The fill color depends on the type of plant (see colors in the second tab on the left or click on any circle on the map). The size indicates the amount of CO or CO2 available.
Chemical plants are blue and polyol plants green. Those are potential sites for the Carbon4PUR technology. CO and CO2 have to be available nearby, and the size of the circle symbolizes the radius (defined by the user) in which transporting those gases might be economical.

Usage

You can add filters to narrow down the emissions:
  • to those compatible with the Carbon4PUR process or by emission type and source ( tab on the left),
  • and to those near an existing chemical plant ( tab).

Data, Licences, Privacy

Data

All emissions have been obtained from E-PRTR. The data was queried via the SPARQL-Endpoint http://semantic.eea.europa.eu/sparql. The exact query can be found here. Some errors have been corrected manually, e.g. the coordinates of Fjernvarme Fyn power plant was changed from somewhere in Cameroon to the correct location in Denmark.
All chemical plants and polyol plants have been gathered manually via desk research, publications and interviews. There is no promise that this data is complete or correct. If you have suggestions, please contact us.

NACE categories and plant types

Carbon4PUR is investigating two cleaning technologies: chemical looping and catalytic cleaning. They differ in their compatibility with input gases. We therefore defined a list of compatible processes for each cleaning step.
catalytic cleaning is compatible with steel gases and the Acheson process.
chemical looping is compatible with steel gases, the Acheson process, biogas before and after combustion, [pyrolysis gas, refineries and steam crackers] all after partial combustion. Cement/lime kiln and ammonia could be used if the off gases were separated and then mixed with CO, H2 or CH4 containing streams .
To get an idea of where those emissions are available, the NACE category for each of those processes have been obtained:
Steel: 24.10 Manufacture of basic iron and steel and of ferro-alloys
Acheson: 20.13 Manufacture of other inorganic basic chemicals
Biogas: 35.11 Production of electricity
Pyrolysis: 06.20 Extraction of natural gas
Refineries and steam crackers: 19.20 Manufacture of refined petroleum products
Cement: 23.51 Manufacture of cement
Lime and Plaster: 23.52 Manufacture of lime and plaster
Ammonia: 20.15 Manufacture of fertilisers and nitrogen compounds
